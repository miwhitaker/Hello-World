Four Fundamental Forces

Every force in the universe can be grouped into one of the following four forces (ordered from weakest to strongest):

  1. Gravity
  2. Electromagnetic
  3. Weak Nuclear Force
  4. Strong Nuclear Force

Most people are at least somewhat familiar with the first two, but the other two are relatively unknown to most.

Gravity

This is the force we all learn about in school. It's what keeps our feet on the ground and the earth in orbit around the sun.

Electromagnetic

This is the force responsible for the electrical power we all use and the force that keeps your feet from falling through the floor. Most people have seen magnets and used electrical power, but didn't realize they are like the head or tails on a coin: different manifestations of the same force.

Weak Nuclear Force

Beta decay

This is a lesser known force that only acts at short range near the nucleus of an atom. It is responsible for some types of nuclear radiation, such as beta rays (a neutron decaying into a proton, electron and an antineutrino)
